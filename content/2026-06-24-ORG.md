## U.S. National News

1. **Senate votes to halt U.S. military action against Iran.** The Senate voted 50–48 for a war powers resolution directing President Trump to halt military action against Iran, after the House passed a similar measure earlier this month. Reuters reported that it was the first time both chambers had passed such a resolution under the 1973 War Powers Act, though its legal force remains contested. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-senate-joins-house-voting-halt-iran-war-rebuking-trump-2026-06-23/)

2. **Federal judge blocks proof-of-citizenship voting requirement.** A federal judge in Boston permanently barred the Trump administration from enforcing parts of an election executive order that would require documentary proof of citizenship to register to vote. The Associated Press reported that the ruling found election regulation belongs to states and Congress, not the president. [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/358912bcb6c7223b3d2d36465156fde9)

3. **Trump presses Senate Republicans on voter ID legislation.** President Trump canceled a planned signing of a bipartisan affordable housing bill and urged Senate Republicans to move first on the SAVE America Act, which would require photo ID and proof of citizenship for federal elections. Reuters reported that the proposal faces the Senate’s 60-vote threshold despite Republican control of the chamber. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/senate-visit-trump-push-voter-id-bill-that-republicans-say-cant-pass-2026-06-24/)

4. **Supreme Court nears major decisions on presidential power.** Reuters reported that the Supreme Court is nearing the end of its term with several cases involving President Trump’s use of executive authority, including birthright citizenship, agency removals, and immigration protections. The pending rulings could shape the legal limits of presidential power heading into the midterm elections. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/trumps-power-takes-center-stage-us-supreme-courts-home-stretch-2026-06-24/)

5. **America 250 kickoff becomes a campaign-style event.** President Trump launched America’s 250th anniversary celebrations with a rally on the National Mall, including patriotic performances, military elements, and a presidential speech. AP described the event as a political-style gathering rather than a traditional nonpartisan national commemoration. [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/75e2bb4f4d2b3f7ab8cdddb86879bec7)

## International News

1. **U.S. and Iran dispute nuclear inspection terms.** Reuters reported that the United States and Iran remain at odds over whether Tehran has agreed to international nuclear inspections and how unfrozen Iranian assets may be used under the interim peace framework. IAEA chief Rafael Grossi said inspections will go ahead, while Iranian officials said access to sensitive or attacked sites must wait for a final deal. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/us-waives-iran-sanctions-trump-says-he-will-do-what-i-have-to-if-tehran-2026-06-23/) | [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/iaea-chief-says-iran-inspections-will-go-ahead-working-modalities-2026-06-24/)

2. **UN inquiry accuses Israel of targeting Gaza children.** A United Nations inquiry said Israeli authorities and security forces deliberately targeted Palestinian children in Gaza, amounting to genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes. Reuters reported that Israel rejected the findings as defamatory and biased. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/israel-targeted-gaza-children-resulting-genocide-un-inquiry-says-2026-06-23/)

3. **Deadly Western Europe heatwave breaks records.** Reuters reported that an “Omega” heatwave brought record temperatures to Western Europe, including 44.3°C in Pissos, France, and a June record in Paris. Authorities said at least 48 people in France drowned while seeking relief from the heat, and Italy placed 16 cities under maximum heat alerts. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/power-cuts-france-leave-thousands-sweltering-amid-scorching-heatwave-2026-06-24/)

4. **Congo Ebola outbreak continues to outpace response.** The World Health Organization said the Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo remains fast-moving, with more than 1,000 infections and health workers facing threats. Reuters reported this is the highest first-month case total recorded for any Ebola outbreak. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/healthcare-pharmaceuticals/ebola-outbreak-is-still-outpacing-response-whos-tedros-says-2026-06-24/)

5. **France confirms Ebola case in returning doctor.** France reported an Ebola case in a doctor who returned from a humanitarian mission in Congo. Reuters reported that the patient is isolated, contact tracing is underway, and health officials said the wider European risk remains low. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/healthcare-pharmaceuticals/france-reports-ebola-case-doctor-returning-humanitarian-mission-congo-2026-06-24/)

## Technology Industry News

1. **Micron earnings are a major test for chip stocks.** Reuters reported that investors were closely watching Micron Technology’s earnings after a volatile stretch for semiconductor shares. Micron’s large gains over the past year have been driven by AI-related memory-chip demand, but analysts warned expectations are very high. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/nervous-investors-await-micron-earnings-chip-sector-whipsaws-2026-06-24/)

2. **SK Hynix plans a large U.S. listing.** South Korea’s SK Hynix said it aims to raise up to $29.4 billion through a U.S. ADR listing on Nasdaq. Reuters reported that the company plans to use the proceeds for chip factory expansion and equipment as demand for AI memory chips grows. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/south-koreas-sk-hynix-says-raise-29-bln-us-adr-listing-2026-06-24/)

3. **Chip selloff highlights AI valuation concerns.** Reuters reported that U.S. chip stocks fell sharply Tuesday, with the semiconductor index dropping 7.9% and Micron, Qualcomm, Marvell, and Nvidia all down. Bargain-hunting later limited the broader technology rout, but the move showed how sensitive markets are to AI trade expectations. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/media-telecom/nasdaq-100-set-shed-over-1-trillion-tech-selloff-deepens-spacex-slides-2026-06-23/)

4. **China frames its technology growth as an opportunity.** China’s Premier Li Qiang defended the country’s technology advances as an opportunity for the world rather than a threat. AP reported the remarks as global concerns continue over subsidies, artificial intelligence, and China’s industrial policy. [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/china-economy-subsidies-ai-tech-li-wef-497753eb03fbe61ccd192782d72d59be)

## Artificial Intelligence News

1. **AI memory demand drives SK Hynix’s market move.** SK Hynix’s planned Nasdaq listing is tied directly to strong demand for high-bandwidth memory used in AI data centers. Reuters reported that customers include Nvidia and Google and that investor appetite for AI-linked chip companies remains strong despite recent volatility. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/south-koreas-sk-hynix-says-raise-29-bln-us-adr-listing-2026-06-24/)

2. **Micron becomes a bellwether for AI infrastructure spending.** Reuters reported that Micron’s results are being watched as a signal of whether AI-related semiconductor demand remains strong enough to support high valuations. The company’s memory chips are central to data-center AI systems. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/nervous-investors-await-micron-earnings-chip-sector-whipsaws-2026-06-24/)

3. **Google faces pressure after AI talent departures.** Reuters reported that John Jumper, a senior Google DeepMind scientist and AlphaFold co-creator, is leaving for Anthropic. The move follows other high-profile AI talent shifts and reflects intense competition among leading AI companies. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/us-scientist-john-jumper-leave-google-deepmind-anthropic-2026-06-19/)

4. **European companies diversify AI providers amid U.S. restrictions.** Reuters reported that limits on access to some U.S. AI services are pushing European firms to spread risk across multiple providers and consider more domestic alternatives. The restrictions include U.S. government limits affecting Anthropic model access for foreign nationals. [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/litigation/us-curbs-ai-spur-european-firms-spread-risk-2026-06-22/)

5. **Anthropic model restrictions remain part of the AI policy debate.** AP reported earlier this month that Anthropic took its Fable 5 and Mythos 5 models offline to comply with a Trump administration directive restricting access by foreign nationals. The decision continues to shape discussion over AI access, national security, and global competition. [Associated Press](https://apnews.com/article/anthropic-artificial-intelligence-trump-fable-mythos-d9cc7df5c02e93837d0f0bfb24d5cfd2)
