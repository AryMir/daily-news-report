## U.S. National News
### Kentucky flooding kills at least four
Severe thunderstorms and flash flooding killed at least four people in Kentucky, where emergency crews were still responding to high water and storm damage on Sunday. AP reported that the flooding followed a fresh round of heavy rain across already vulnerable areas.

### Three firefighters die on the Colorado-Utah border
Three wildland firefighters were killed while battling a lightning-caused fire near the Colorado-Utah border, and two others were injured. The deaths came as dangerous wind and dry conditions continued to strain fire crews across the West.

### Supreme Court revives path for restrictive asylum metering
The Supreme Court ruled 6-3 that the Trump administration can potentially revive a restrictive border policy that limits how many asylum seekers are processed each day. The decision gives the administration new room to turn back migrants at the U.S.-Mexico border while the broader case continues.

### Inflation gauge hits a three-year high
The Commerce Department's latest PCE report showed consumer prices up 4.1% from a year earlier in May, the highest annual reading since April 2023. AP also noted that the average 30-year fixed mortgage rate rose to 6.88%, adding to affordability pressure for households.

### New Mexico opens criminal probe into DEA fentanyl handling
New Mexico Attorney General Raul Torrez launched a criminal investigation after AP reporting showed DEA agents allowed large fentanyl shipments to reach Albuquerque streets while trying to build larger cases. The dispute has intensified scrutiny of federal tactics in one of the states hardest hit by overdose deaths.

## International News
### Iran attacks Bahrain and Kuwait after U.S. strikes
Iran launched drone and missile attacks on Bahrain and Kuwait on Sunday after recent U.S. strikes, damaging a residential building in Bahrain and prompting interceptions in Kuwait. Tehran also threatened to halt talks aimed at ending the wider war.

### Ukraine's drone campaign hits another Russian refinery
Ukraine said its drone assault set a major refinery ablaze in Slavyansk-na-Kubani in southern Russia, part of a strategy to hit Moscow's energy and military infrastructure. Russian authorities said the attack killed one person and injured another nearby.

### France reports about 1,000 additional heat-wave deaths
French health authorities said the country's record-setting heat wave was linked to around 1,000 additional deaths in only three days, with about 85% of the victims aged 65 and older. The broader European heat emergency also brought fires, transport disruption, and new weather records.

### Venezuela's earthquake death toll reaches 1,430
Venezuela's twin earthquakes have now killed at least 1,430 people, and AP reported that frustration is rising as relatives search for the missing and accuse authorities of responding too slowly. International rescue teams are on scene, but time remains critical for survivors trapped in rubble.

### Burkina Faso severs diplomatic ties with France
Burkina Faso's military government cut diplomatic relations with France effective immediately, accusing Paris of neo-colonial ambitions and support for subversive networks. France called the decision hostile and unfounded, underscoring the continuing geopolitical shift across the Sahel.

## Technology Industry News
### U.S. pressure grows for tougher rules on kids and social media
AP reported Sunday that the U.S. still trails several other countries in restricting children's access to social media, but the political push for tougher rules is growing. Families affected by online harms are pressing lawmakers for broader platform limits and accountability.

### ATF cancels a phone-tracking contract after legal concerns
The ATF canceled its contract for Webloc, a surveillance tool that used commercial location data to track phones without warrants. AP reported the change followed bipartisan concerns from lawmakers, as well as objections raised in court over the tool's legality.

### Former Meta executive sues over memoir gag order
Former Meta executive Sarah Wynn-Williams sued Meta in federal court, arguing the company is unlawfully trying to silence her after publication of her memoir *Careless People*. The lawsuit challenges Meta's arbitration order and severance terms, while Meta says she violated her agreement.

### Alphabet is set to join the Dow on Monday
MarketWatch reported that Alphabet will replace Verizon in the Dow Jones Industrial Average effective June 29. The move further shifts the index away from its older industrial and telecom mix toward a more tech-heavy lineup.

## Artificial Intelligence News
### OpenAI and Anthropic restrict their newest models
OpenAI and Anthropic said their latest frontier models will be available only to a small group of Trump-approved customers during a federal cybersecurity review. AP reported that OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol and Anthropic's Mythos 5 are now being released under unusually tight access controls.

### New bipartisan group launches to address AI job losses
A new nonprofit called RAISE US launched with more than $500 million to help workers retrain for AI-driven job disruption. AP said the effort is backed by former Commerce Secretary Gina Raimondo and former Indiana Gov. Eric Holcomb and will work with states and major employers.

### Deepfake case brings more legal scrutiny to AI in politics
Former New York City Council candidate Jonathan Rinaldi was charged with forgery over AI-generated fake endorsements and fabricated news content posted during his campaign. The case highlights how older fraud laws are now being applied to political deepfakes.

### Environmental pressure on AI companies keeps rising
The United Nations is urging AI companies to publish their environmental impact and commit to clean energy as data-center power and water use climb. AP reported that the growing footprint of AI systems is becoming a bigger policy issue alongside questions about jobs and safety.
