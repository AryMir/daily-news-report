## U.S. National News
### Inflation gauge hits a three-year high
The Commerce Department's latest report showed the PCE price index up 4.1% from a year earlier in May, the highest since April 2023. Core PCE, which strips out food and energy, rose 3.4%, reinforcing concerns that the Fed may keep rates high or even consider further tightening.

### White House asks Congress for a new war and emergency funding package
The White House formally requested $87.6 billion in supplemental spending, largely to replenish Pentagon costs tied to the U.S. war against Iran. The package also includes aid for farmers and money for the Ebola outbreak in Central Africa, setting up a difficult fight on Capitol Hill.

### Supreme Court allows protections to end for Haitians and Syrians under TPS
In a 6-3 ruling, the Supreme Court allowed the Trump administration to end Temporary Protected Status for Haitians and Syrians. The decision directly affects roughly 356,000 people from those two countries and could shape broader cases involving TPS holders from other nations.

### Judge holds prosecutors in Charlie Kirk murder case in contempt
A Utah judge held prosecutors in contempt for comments they made to the media about Tyler Robinson, the defendant charged in the killing of Charlie Kirk. The judge said the remarks crossed a gag-order line, though he declined to remove the death penalty as a possible punishment.

### Samuel Bateman convicted on Arizona child-abuse charges
Samuel Bateman, already serving a 50-year federal sentence in a separate sexual-abuse case, was convicted on Arizona child-abuse charges after three girls were found in an enclosed trailer he was towing. Sentencing in the Arizona case is scheduled for August 25.

## International News
### Rescue window narrows after Venezuela's twin earthquakes
Venezuela entered a critical rescue phase after back-to-back earthquakes devastated coastal and capital areas. The death toll has climbed above 900, thousands have been injured, and rescuers are still searching collapsed buildings as aid trickles in.

### Israel and Lebanon sign a U.S.-backed framework agreement
Secretary of State Marco Rubio joined Israeli and Lebanese officials in Washington for the signing of a framework agreement described as a first step toward peace after months of conflict with Hezbollah. The agreement outlines a path toward Hezbollah disarmament and phased territorial handoffs, but Hezbollah has rejected it.

### Britain remains in political transition after Keir Starmer's resignation
Britain is still dealing with the aftermath of Prime Minister Keir Starmer's June 22 resignation. Andy Burnham is the sole contender to replace him and is expected to formally take office in July, but he would inherit Starmer-era fiscal constraints and a difficult domestic agenda.

### German court gives life sentence in Magdeburg market attack case
A German court convicted Taleb al-Abdulmohsen, a Saudi doctor, of murder and other charges in the 2024 car-ramming attack on the Magdeburg Christmas market. The court imposed a life sentence and found the guilt especially severe, making early parole highly unlikely.

### Two tropical storms hit Japan with floods and landslides
Storms Mekkhala and Higos brought flooding, landslides, and transport disruption to Japan on Saturday. At least one person was reported dead, more than 30 homes were flooded, and rail and air service were disrupted in western Japan.

## Technology Industry News
### onsemi moves to buy Synaptics
onsemi and Synaptics announced a definitive all-stock deal that values Synaptics at about $7 billion in enterprise value. The companies said the combination is aimed at building more capable intelligent systems for physical AI.

### Micron reports record quarterly results
Micron reported record fiscal third-quarter 2026 results on June 24, with revenue of $41.46 billion versus $23.86 billion in the prior quarter and $9.30 billion a year earlier. The company said the surge reflects exceptional demand across memory products tied to AI infrastructure.

### Apple raises prices on Macs and iPads
TechCrunch reported that Apple raised prices across several Mac, iPad, HomePod, and Apple TV products while leaving iPhone pricing unchanged for now. The report points to a broader repricing across Apple's hardware lineup rather than a single-device adjustment.

### OpenAI and Broadcom unveil a custom inference chip
OpenAI and Broadcom announced Jalapeno, an LLM-optimized inference chip designed to improve speed, efficiency, and scale for advanced model serving. OpenAI described it as the first accelerator in a planned multi-generation compute platform.

## Artificial Intelligence News
### OpenAI previews GPT-5.6 Sol
OpenAI on June 26 previewed GPT-5.6 Sol as its new flagship model, highlighting stronger coding, biology, and cybersecurity performance along with a more advanced safeguard stack. The company also introduced new reasoning settings, including a deeper "max" mode and an "ultra" mode that can use subagents.

### OpenAI confirms a confidential S-1 filing
OpenAI said it has submitted a confidential draft S-1 to the SEC, giving it the option to go public sooner if it decides that is strategically useful. The company said it has not yet determined timing for any further action.

### OpenAI and Anthropic restrict newest models during a cybersecurity review
The Associated Press reported Friday that OpenAI and Anthropic are limiting access to their latest frontier models to Trump-approved customers while a federal cybersecurity review proceeds. That makes Washington a direct factor in how quickly the newest high-end AI systems reach wider users.

### Anthropic model reportedly found vulnerabilities in classified systems
AP also reported this week that Anthropic's Mythos model identified vulnerabilities in classified U.S. government computer systems during a test run with intelligence agencies. Officials said the model found the weaknesses quickly, intensifying debate over both defensive uses and control of advanced AI.

### Anthropic publishes a new Economic Index report
Anthropic published a new Economic Index report, "Cadences," on June 26. The report adds fresh data to the fast-moving debate over how AI is changing work patterns, task mix, and the pace at which knowledge work is being automated or reshaped.
