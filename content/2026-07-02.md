# Daily News Report | July 2, 2026

## U.S. National News

1. **U.S. job growth slowed sharply in June.** The Labor Department reported 57,000 new nonfarm payroll jobs in June, below expectations, while unemployment was 4.2% and labor-force participation fell to 61.5%.

2. **Judge blocked proposed USPS mail-in ballot restrictions.** Reuters reported that a federal judge blocked a U.S. Postal Service proposal to refuse ballot deliveries from states that did not meet new requirements, saying the proposal violated a 2021 settlement with the NAACP.

3. **Trump administration proposed a Medicare drug-payment rule.** The administration proposed changing reimbursement for hospitals in the 340B drug-discount program, saying it could save Medicare patients $1.1 billion next year.

4. **$46 billion “smart wall” project advanced at the U.S.-Mexico border.** AP reported that the administration is moving ahead with a border-security program combining 30-foot steel fencing with sensors, cameras, towers, and artificial-intelligence tools.

5. **A major heat wave affected large parts of the United States before July 4.** Reuters and AP reported dangerous heat across many states, with special concern for holiday travel, outdoor events, and vulnerable residents.

## International News

1. **Russia launched a major missile and drone attack on Kyiv.** Reuters reported that hundreds of drones and dozens of missiles struck Ukraine’s capital, killing at least 21 people, injuring many more, and damaging about 130 buildings.

2. **U.S.-Iran talks in Doha focused on the Strait of Hormuz.** Reuters reported that talks concluded in Qatar with attention on shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, frozen Iranian assets, and the path toward a broader deal.

3. **Wildfires burned in southern France after extreme heat and drought.** AP reported fires in the Aude and Herault regions, with about 800 firefighters and 150 vehicles deployed and evacuations from campsites near Canet-en-Roussillon.

4. **China and the European Union agreed to hold regular trade talks.** Reuters and AP reported that China invited EU trade chief Maroš Šefčovič to China in the fall as both sides seek to manage trade tensions and Europe’s large trade deficit with China.

5. **Papua separatists said they killed an American pilot in Indonesia.** Reuters and AP reported that separatist rebels in Indonesia’s Papua region claimed responsibility for shooting an American pilot and burning a civilian aircraft; Indonesian officials disputed claims that troops were being transported.
