## U.S. National News

1. **U.S. job growth slowed sharply in June.** The Labor Department reported 57,000 new nonfarm payroll jobs, compared with economists’ expectations for 110,000, while April and May were revised down by a combined 74,000 jobs. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-job-growth-misses-expectations-june-unemployment-rate-falls-42-2026-07-02/))

2. **The unemployment rate fell for a weak reason.** Unemployment dropped to 4.2%, but Reuters reported that the decline was driven by about 720,000 people leaving the labor force, pushing participation to its lowest level since March 2021. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/us-job-growth-misses-expectations-june-unemployment-rate-falls-42-2026-07-02/))

3. **Supreme Court upheld birthright citizenship earlier this week.** The Court rejected President Trump’s executive order that sought to limit citizenship for children born in the United States to parents who are in the country illegally or temporarily. ([AP](https://apnews.com/article/c73cf0c70bb550ebf0a55fafddbd935c))

4. **A federal judge blocked proposed USPS mail-in voting restrictions.** Judge Emmet Sullivan ruled that the Postal Service’s proposed changes violated a 2021 settlement requiring expedited handling of mail ballots through 2028. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/judge-blocks-us-postal-services-proposed-restrictions-mail-in-voting-2026-07-01/))

5. **The U.S. declined to extend the USMCA trade agreement in its current form.** The decision starts a 10-year countdown toward expiration while the administration seeks changes to the North American trade pact. ([Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/americas/us-canada-mexico-review-trade-pact-likely-putting-it-into-limbo-trump-demands-2026-07-01/))
