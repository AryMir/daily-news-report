# Daily News Report | June 25, 2026

## Executive Summary

- The U.S. Supreme Court issued several major rulings today, including decisions on asylum processing, Temporary Protected Status, gun rights on private property, and Roundup-related lawsuits.
- U.S. stocks closed mixed: the Dow rose slightly, the S&P 500 was nearly flat, and the Nasdaq fell as Apple weighed on the technology sector.
- AI-related chip demand remained a central market theme, with Micron rising after strong results and Qualcomm expanding its AI data-center push.
- Internationally, rescue work continued after deadly earthquakes in Venezuela, while Japan reported a strong offshore earthquake with no tsunami warning.
- Henderson, Nevada is hot and dry today, with a Red Flag Warning and Wind Advisory in effect for Friday into Saturday.

## U.S. National News

- **Supreme Court allows restrictive asylum policy to move forward.** The court cleared the way for the Trump administration to potentially revive a border "metering" policy that can turn back some asylum seekers at the U.S.-Mexico border. Source: [AP / Guardian pickup](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2026/jun/25/supreme-court-ruling-asylum-seekers-us-mexico-border).

- **Supreme Court lets administration end protections for Haitians and Syrians.** The justices allowed the administration to end Temporary Protected Status for migrants from Haiti and Syria, a decision affecting more than 350,000 people. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/immigration-supreme-court-haiti-syria-tps-1bbbf8115f984a0d53336656924e989d).

- **Supreme Court strikes down Hawaii gun law.** The court invalidated a Hawaii law requiring permission before carrying guns into stores and hotels, ruling against the state's private-property default rule. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/supreme-court-guns-hawaii-trump-c5dbdf945bc870f70a03455f5eb1dec7).

- **Supreme Court sides with Roundup maker Bayer.** The court ruled in favor of Bayer in a case expected to block thousands of state lawsuits alleging Roundup failed to warn users about cancer risks. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/supreme-court-roundup-monsanto-a7f054d80919f98bdfc5190013a8f6f1).

- **Senate Republicans reject Iran war-powers resolution.** Senate Republicans rejected a war-powers measure after President Trump pressed party members over opposition to his Iran policy; AP reported the vote as 47-50-1. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/senate-republicans-trump-vote-reject-war-powers-0f1fa8189c275188a71ed02cc8c3270d).

## International News

- **Venezuela searches for survivors after two deadly earthquakes.** AP reported that 7.2- and 7.5-magnitude earthquakes killed at least 188 people, injured about 1,500, and left more than 200 trapped. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/venezuela-earthquake-caracas-rodriguez-aid-0a62e6fc9feb5202a750c4fbb11a6aec).

- **Japan reports 7.2 earthquake with no tsunami warning.** A strong quake struck off northern Japan's coast Thursday; authorities reported no tsunami danger and no serious injuries. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/japan-earthquake-tsunami-d55ef22e943aba927183ca016aa486e6).

- **U.N. nuclear chief says inspectors will visit Iranian enrichment sites.** IAEA Director General Rafael Grossi said site visits are part of the interim U.S.-Iran deal, while Iranian officials said decisions would come only within a final agreement. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/iran-us-israel-lebanon-june-24-2026-nuclear-grossi-ceasefire-875ee115cacd1f5923052b70f2be4124).

- **Western Europe faces extreme heat.** AP reported temperatures above 40 C in parts of western Europe, including France, Spain, Belgium, the Netherlands, and the United Kingdom. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/heat-wave-europe-numbers-594f73db651f9683c43acf04e009d5e7).

- **Aid and infrastructure concerns follow Venezuela's quakes.** Acting President Delcy Rodriguez declared a state of emergency and pledged funds for rebuilding as damaged infrastructure complicated relief efforts. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/7179acaee70a9c543f953852f15d4814).

## Technology Industry News

- **Micron rises after strong AI-driven results.** AP reported Micron Technology helped lead the market after quarterly profit and revenue beat analyst expectations, with shares up more than 16%. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/119e56cb6b1fc041b46af9bb778bdf07).

- **Apple raises Mac and iPad prices.** Apple increased prices on Macs and iPads, citing a global memory-chip shortage driven by AI data-center demand; AP reported Apple shares fell 4.5%. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/fe95fe57dfa9b4a9917d68df5dcfe0e3).

- **Qualcomm expands AI data-center plans.** Reuters reported that Qualcomm said Microsoft and Meta will use its new AI chips, and that it will make custom chips for two other unnamed hyperscalers. Source: [Reuters via Investing.com](https://au.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/qualcomm-says-microsoft-meta-will-use-its-new-ai-chips-4503186).

- **Microsoft raises Xbox console prices.** Microsoft said Xbox console prices will rise by $100 for 512 GB models and $150 for 1 TB models starting August 1, and that it will discontinue the 2 TB model. Source: [Xbox Wire](https://news.xbox.com/en-us/2026/06/25/xbox-console-price-update/).

## Artificial Intelligence News

- **AI demand drives memory-chip pressure.** Apple linked its Mac and iPad price increases to higher memory costs caused by AI data-center expansion, a sign of how AI infrastructure demand is reaching consumer electronics. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/fe95fe57dfa9b4a9917d68df5dcfe0e3).

- **Micron's results reinforce the AI chip trade.** AP said Micron's strong results were fueled by AI-related demand for memory chips, helping support shares tied to the AI buildout. Source: [AP](https://apnews.com/article/119e56cb6b1fc041b46af9bb778bdf07).

- **Qualcomm signs major AI-chip customers.** Qualcomm said Microsoft and Meta will use its new AI chips as the company tries to grow beyond handsets and into data-center computing. Source: [Reuters via Investing.com](https://au.investing.com/news/stock-market-news/qualcomm-says-microsoft-meta-will-use-its-new-ai-chips-4503186).

- **Anthropic accuses Alibaba-linked operators of model extraction.** Reports citing an Anthropic letter said Alibaba-linked operators used nearly 25,000 accounts and about 28.8 million Claude interactions in an alleged distillation campaign; Alibaba had not responded in the cited reports. Source: [Business Insider](https://www.businessinsider.com/anthropic-china-alibaba-exploiting-ai-models-distillation-attack-2026-6).
