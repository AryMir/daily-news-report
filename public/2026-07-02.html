2026-07-02

Executive Summary

  • U.S. job growth slowed sharply in June, with employers adding 57,000 jobs and unemployment at 4.2%, giving markets another reason to reassess the Federal Reserve’s next move.
  • A federal judge blocked a U.S. Postal Service proposal that would have restricted mail-in ballot deliveries, while the Supreme Court’s recent mail-ballot ruling remains central to election-law debates.
  • Russia launched one of its largest attacks on Kyiv, killing at least 21 people and damaging about 130 buildings.
  • Oil fell to four-month lows after U.S.-Iran talks in Doha reduced some fears about Strait of Hormuz supply disruptions.
  • Henderson, Nevada is sunny and hot today, with temperatures near 100°F and dry, breezy conditions.

U.S. National News

  1. U.S. job growth slowed sharply in June. The Labor Department reported 57,000 new nonfarm payroll jobs in June, below expectations, while unemployment was 4.2% and labor-force participation fell to 61.5%.
  2. Judge blocked proposed USPS mail-in ballot restrictions. Reuters reported that a federal judge blocked a U.S. Postal Service proposal to refuse ballot deliveries from states that did not meet new requirements, saying the proposal violated a 2021 settlement with the NAACP.
  3. Trump administration proposed a Medicare drug-payment rule. The administration proposed changing reimbursement for hospitals in the 340B drug-discount program, saying it could save Medicare patients $1.1 billion next year.
  4. $46 billion “smart wall” project advanced at the U.S.-Mexico border. AP reported that the administration is moving ahead with a border-security program combining 30-foot steel fencing with sensors, cameras, towers, and artificial-intelligence tools.
  5. A major heat wave affected large parts of the United States before July 4. Reuters and AP reported dangerous heat across many states, with special concern for holiday travel, outdoor events, and vulnerable residents.

International News

  1. Russia launched a major missile and drone attack on Kyiv. Reuters reported that hundreds of drones and dozens of missiles struck Ukraine’s capital, killing at least 21 people, injuring many more, and damaging about 130 buildings.
  2. U.S.-Iran talks in Doha focused on the Strait of Hormuz. Reuters reported that talks concluded in Qatar with attention on shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, frozen Iranian assets, and the path toward a broader deal.
  3. Wildfires burned in southern France after extreme heat and drought. AP reported fires in the Aude and Herault regions, with about 800 firefighters and 150 vehicles deployed and evacuations from campsites near Canet-en-Roussillon.
  4. China and the European Union agreed to hold regular trade talks. Reuters and AP reported that China invited EU trade chief Maroš Šefčovič to China in the fall as both sides seek to manage trade tensions and Europe’s large trade deficit with China.
  5. Papua separatists said they killed an American pilot in Indonesia. Reuters and AP reported that separatist rebels in Indonesia’s Papua region claimed responsibility for shooting an American pilot and burning a civilian aircraft; Indonesian officials disputed claims that troops were being transported.

Technology and chip shares remained under pressure, while oil declined as diplomatic progress around U.S.-Iran talks eased some fears about supply disruptions.

Technology Industry News

  1. Tesla reported record second-quarter deliveries. Reuters reported that Tesla delivered 480,126 vehicles in Q2 2026, above Wall Street estimates, helped by improved European demand, although the stock fell as investors weighed valuation and future-growth questions.
  2. Meta explored selling excess AI computing capacity. Reuters reported that Meta is building a cloud business that could let developers use AI models and computing power hosted on Meta infrastructure.
  3. SoftBank renewed talks for a $10 billion loan backed by its OpenAI stake. Reuters reported that SoftBank reopened discussions with lenders and offered additional guarantees to support its AI investment strategy.
  4. Rivian raised its 2026 delivery forecast. Reuters reported that Rivian increased its expected annual deliveries to 65,000–70,000 vehicles, helped by demand for its R1 vehicles and the newly launched R2 SUV.
  5. Semiconductor stocks remained under pressure. Reuters reported that the U.S. chip index fell sharply as investors questioned whether AI-related computing capacity has been overbuilt.

Artificial Intelligence News

  1. The U.S. government is discussing voluntary AI model standards. Reuters reported that U.S. officials are in talks with AI companies about voluntary standards for releasing new models, with an announcement possible soon.
  2. OpenAI reportedly proposed a 5% U.S. government stake. Reuters, citing the Financial Times, reported that OpenAI proposed giving the U.S. government a 5% stake as part of broader discussions tied to its future structure and public offering plans.
  3. A U.N. panel warned about risks from unchecked AI development. Reuters reported that a 40-expert U.N. scientific panel said AI capabilities are advancing faster than scientific understanding and government oversight.
  4. Portugal launched its first open-source AI model. Reuters reported that Portugal introduced “Amalia,” an open-source national AI model backed by universities, research institutions, and EU recovery funds.
  5. AI dominated discussion among global central bankers. Reuters reported that central bankers at the ECB forum in Portugal discussed both AI’s productivity potential and risks such as market volatility, cybersecurity threats, inequality, and opaque automated decisions.
