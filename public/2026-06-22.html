U.S. National News

  • U.S. and Iranian officials concluded their initial high-level talks in Switzerland, with mediators from Pakistan and Qatar reporting "encouraging progress" on a peace roadmap.
  • A major dispute threatens the U.S.-Iran deal over billions in frozen Tehran funds, according to experts.
  • U.S. Vice President JD Vance confirmed that President Trump's earlier threats on the Strait of Hormuz briefly disrupted negotiations but talks ultimately continued.
  • The U.S. military has resumed airstrikes against al-Shabab militants in Somalia, ending a brief lull in operations.
  • The aircraft carrier USS George Washington and its escorts have joined Japanese warships for an exercise in the Philippine Sea.

International News

  • A Russian drone strike on the city of Sumy in northeastern Ukraine killed three members of the same family, including a 13-year-old boy.
  • Confirmed Ebola cases in the ongoing Congo outbreak have now surpassed 1,000, with 254 reported deaths, according to authorities.
  • UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer announced his resignation after facing increasing pressure and a contentious period following Brexit.
  • Europe is grappling with a record-breaking heatwave, with some regions expecting temperatures to reach up to 44°C (111°F).
  • Four Qatari liquefied natural gas (LNG) tankers entered the Strait of Hormuz today, navigating the critical waterway despite recent tensions and shipping slowdowns.

Artificial Intelligence News

  • The U.S. government ordered Anthropic to disable its most advanced AI models, Fable 5 and Mythos 5, for foreign nationals worldwide due to national security concerns regarding potential exploitation of software vulnerabilities.
  • G7 leaders are discussing a potential "trusted-partners" framework to allow approved countries or organizations access to advanced U.S. AI models, addressing concerns about recent restrictions.
  • More than 100 cybersecurity experts and industry leaders have urged the U.S. government to lift export controls on Anthropic's AI models, arguing that limiting access could hinder defensive capabilities.
  • OpenAI has launched a new business unit, the OpenAI Deployment Company, backed by over $4 billion, to assist organizations in integrating AI systems into their critical workflows and operations.
