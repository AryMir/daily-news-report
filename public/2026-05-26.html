National Headlines

  • Texas GOP Senate Runoff Concludes: Voters in Texas headed to the polls for a key Republican Senate runoff between four-term incumbent John Cornyn and State Attorney General Ken Paxton. The race is seen as a battle for the future direction of the Republican party, with former President Trump endorsing Paxton.
  • California Chemical Emergency Averted: Officials in Garden Grove, California, have lifted evacuation orders after determining the risk of a major explosion at a GKN aerospace facility has been averted. Crews worked to cool an overheating tank of flammable chemicals, and pressure was relieved after a crack formed in the tank.

  • Quad Foreign Ministers Meet in Delhi: Foreign Ministers from the U.S., India, Australia, and Japan are meeting in New Delhi to discuss regional security and cooperation. U.S. Secretary of State Marco Rubio is in attendance.
  • WHO Warns Ebola Outbreak Outpacing Response: The World Health Organization stated that a fast-moving Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo and Uganda is growing faster than response efforts. The suspected death toll has now reached 220.
  • Heat Dome Scorches Western Europe: A "heat dome" has brought record-high May temperatures to the U.K., Ireland, France, and Spain. The extreme heat is caused by warm air from North Africa trapped under a high-pressure system.

Technology Industry News

  • Dell Shares Soar on AI Demand: Dell Technologies stock jumped 16.8% to a record high. The surge was fueled by massive demand for its AI servers and an optimistic forecast, signaling strong growth in its AI infrastructure business.

Artificial Intelligence News

  • Big Tech Pours Billions into AI Infrastructure: Alphabet, Amazon, Meta, and Microsoft are collectively spending around $750 billion in 2026 on AI infrastructure. This massive investment comes as some companies, like Meta, simultaneously cut jobs to reallocate resources toward AI development.
  • Pope Issues Encyclical on AI Risks: Pope Leo XIV issued a major encyclical warning that AI must serve humanity. The document calls for government regulation, worker retraining, and safeguards to ensure humans retain control over critical decisions, especially concerning weaponry.
  • Gartner Predicts Legal Tech Budgets to Double by 2028: A new report from Gartner forecasts that legal tech spending will double in the next four years, driven largely by the adoption of specialized legal AI platforms that are automating tasks like contract review and legal research.

The event focused on developing concrete tools for the responsible governance of AI, with a focus on AI literacy and data sovereignty.
