U.S. National News

  • The first 2024 presidential debate between President Biden and former President Trump generated widespread commentary, with polls suggesting a stronger performance by Trump.
  • The Supreme Court rejected a bankruptcy settlement that would have shielded Sackler family members from opioid epidemic lawsuits.
  • In a separate ruling, the Supreme Court curbed the SEC's power to assign penalties via administrative law judges, requiring a jury trial instead.
  • Reports indicate the Biden administration is preparing to lift a de facto ban on Pentagon contractors deploying to Ukraine, potentially bringing U.S. military personnel closer to direct confrontation with Russia.
  • A mass shooting in Corpus Christi, Texas, resulted in two fatalities and three injuries.

International News

  • Iranians are voting in a snap presidential election following the death of President Ebrahim Raisi last month.
  • More than 20 Kenyan protesters were killed in clashes with security forces over a new tax bill, which President William Ruto has since said he will not sign.
  • The U.S. military dismantled the Gaza floating pier for the third time due to bad weather, impacting humanitarian aid delivery to the region.
  • China has expelled former defense ministers Li Shangfu and Wei Fenghe from the ruling Communist Party amid corruption allegations.
  • A powerful magnitude 7.2 earthquake struck off the coast of central Peru, triggering a tsunami warning for the region and injuring at least five people.
