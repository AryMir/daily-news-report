Technology Industry News

  • Huawei has introduced the "Tau (τ) Scaling Law," a new principle intended to replace Moore's Law for guiding semiconductor development. The company claims this new approach, focusing on time scaling rather than geometric scaling, will lead to significant breakthroughs in transistor density and performance.
  • Google Cloud launched an AI startup innovation corridor connecting Southeast Asia with Silicon Valley. The initiative aims to help startups in the region develop and commercialize AI products with access to Google's latest models, including the Gemini 3.5 family.
  • The Middle East is experiencing an AI infrastructure boom, marked by the accelerated growth of "AI factories" and specialized data centers. This trend is driving massive demand for enterprise storage, servers, and GPUs from companies like NVIDIA, AMD, and Intel.
  • Crypto4A announced the general availability of QxVault, its new quantum-safe secrets management platform designed for enterprise-level security and data sovereignty.

Artificial Intelligence News

  • The Vatican has officially weighed in on AI, with Pope Leo XIV issuing "Magnifica humanitas," the first-ever papal encyclical focused on artificial intelligence. The document calls for strong ethical regulations and warns against the concentration of AI power within a few large corporations.
  • AI firm Anthropic is reportedly on the verge of closing a $30 billion funding round that could value the company at over $900 billion, potentially surpassing OpenAI's valuation for the first time.
  • Fujitsu announced the development of a self-evolving multi-AI agent technology. The system allows teams of AI agents to learn and adapt to new business operations, policy changes, and human feedback continuously.
  • New research from the University of Vaasa suggests that employees who view generative AI tools like ChatGPT and Gemini as helpful collaborators rather than threats are more engaged, adaptable, and optimistic about their careers.
