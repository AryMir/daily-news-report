Executive Summary

  • U.S. inflation moved back above 4% in May, raising pressure on the Federal Reserve as energy costs from the Middle East conflict continued to affect prices.
  • Wall Street opened higher on June 25 after Micron and Qualcomm revived optimism around AI-related chip demand.
  • Oil prices fell back to pre-Iran-war levels as tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz improved.
  • A deadly Western Europe heatwave continued to disrupt daily life, while Venezuela faced a major earthquake disaster.
  • Henderson, Nevada is sunny and very hot today, with temperatures near 109°F this afternoon and a Red Flag Warning beginning Friday.

U.S. National News

1. U.S. inflation tops 4% in May. The Commerce Department’s PCE inflation measure rose 4.1% over the 12 months through May, the first reading above 4% since April 2023. Reuters reported that Middle East-related energy costs were a major factor, even though oil and gasoline prices have eased recently.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/markets/us/us-pce-inflation-measure-tops-40-may-consumer-spending-strong-2026-06-25/)

2. Federal judge blocks new student loan restrictions. A federal judge in Washington, D.C., blocked the Trump administration from implementing lower federal student loan limits for some graduate healthcare programs. The rule had been scheduled to take effect July 1.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/legal/government/us-judge-blocks-trump-administrations-new-student-loan-restrictions-2026-06-25/)

3. Millions have lost SNAP benefits under federal cuts. Reuters reported that more than 4.7 million people nationwide have lost food stamp benefits since the tax and spending law took effect last July. Arizona has seen the steepest drop, with about half of SNAP recipients losing benefits.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/millions-lose-food-stamps-under-trump-cuts-arizona-is-hardest-hit-2026-06-24/)

4. NHTSA proposes easing rules for fully self-driving vehicles. The National Highway Traffic Safety Administration proposed ending the requirement that self-driving vehicles without human controls include manual brake pedals. The agency said braking performance and stopping-distance standards would remain in place.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/us-proposes-drop-brake-pedal-requirements-self-driving-vehicles-2026-06-25/)

5. U.S. denies Polestar authorization to sell future model-year vehicles. Polestar said the U.S. did not grant authorization for it to sell vehicles in the country from model year 2027 onward. The Sweden-based EV maker, majority-owned by China’s Geely, said it would continue selling existing U.S. stock and servicing current vehicles.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/us-denies-polestar-authorization-sell-vehicles-latest-strike-against-china-made-2026-06-25/)

International News

1. Venezuela struck by deadly back-to-back earthquakes. Reuters reported that two major earthquakes hit Venezuela near Caracas, killing at least 164 people, injuring hundreds, and leaving rescue workers searching collapsed buildings. The situation remained active, with officials and international agencies warning the toll could rise.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/venezuela-earthquake-live-high-casualties-likely-after-two-strong-quakes-2026-06-25/)

2. Oil traffic through Hormuz rebounds after Iran-war disruption. Reuters reported that oil prices returned to pre-war levels as the United States said flows through the Strait of Hormuz were close to normal. Officials said complete normalization could still take weeks because of mine-clearance concerns.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/oil-prices-fall-tankers-exit-strait-hormuz-2026-06-25/)

3. Europe’s deadly heatwave disrupts schools, power, and landmarks. Authorities in France and Britain urged residents to change routines as Western Europe remained under a severe heatwave. Paris recorded a June record of 40.9°C, or 105.6°F, on Wednesday.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/environment/europeans-told-protect-themselves-deadly-heatwave-takes-its-toll-2026-06-25/)

4. Kenya police disperse protest anniversary gatherings. Kenyan police used tear gas and roadblocks in Nairobi as people marked the anniversary of deadly 2024 anti-government protests. Families and activists continued to demand accountability for protest-related killings.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/africa/kenyans-march-mark-anniversary-deadly-2024-anti-government-protests-2026-06-25/)

5. U.S. and World Bank agency plan political-risk insurance for Ukraine fund. The U.S. International Development Finance Corporation and the World Bank’s MIGA agreed to set up a political-risk insurance framework to support private investment in Ukraine reconstruction.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/us-dfc-world-banks-miga-set-up-political-risk-insurance-ukraine-fund-2026-06-25/)

Technology Industry News

1. Apple raises iPad and MacBook prices. Apple said it could no longer shield customers from rising memory and storage chip costs tied to AI data-center demand. Reuters reported that the lowest-priced MacBook Neo would rise from $599 to $699, while iPhones were not affected.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/apple-raises-prices-macbooks-ipads-memory-costs-skyrocket-2026-06-25/)

2. EU targets Amazon and Microsoft cloud units for possible gatekeeper rules. EU antitrust regulators said Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure should be designated as “gatekeepers” under the Digital Markets Act. The designation would impose obligations around interoperability, data portability, and limits on self-preferencing.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/amazon-microsoft-cloud-computing-services-should-fall-under-eu-tech-rules-eu-2026-06-25/)

3. IBM unveils sub-1-nanometer chip technology. IBM said its new 0.7-nanometer “nanostack” transistor architecture could improve performance by up to 50% or energy efficiency by 70%. The company said production could begin within five years.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/ibm-unveils-tech-chip-smaller-than-1-nanometer-ai-computing-push-2026-06-25/)

4. Amazon announces more India cloud and AI investment. Amazon said it will invest an additional $13 billion in India by 2030 to expand AI and cloud infrastructure, bringing its planned investment in the country to $48 billion through 2030.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/india/amazon-invest-additional-13-billion-india-2026-06-25/)

5. NHTSA proposes rule changes for self-driving vehicles. The proposed rule would remove manual brake-pedal requirements for vehicles built without human driver controls, while keeping performance rules for braking and stopping distance.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/autos-transportation/us-proposes-drop-brake-pedal-requirements-self-driving-vehicles-2026-06-25/)

Artificial Intelligence News

1. Micron and Qualcomm forecasts reignite AI chip rally. Reuters reported that chipmakers added more than $400 billion in market value after strong forecasts from Micron and Qualcomm. Micron cited AI infrastructure demand for memory chips, while Qualcomm said it expects $15 billion in data-center sales by 2029.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/micron-qualcomm-forecasts-ignite-400-billion-ai-chip-stock-rally-2026-06-24/)

2. Anthropic accuses Alibaba of extracting Claude model capabilities. Anthropic said in a letter seen by Reuters that Alibaba-linked operators generated more than 28.8 million exchanges with Claude through nearly 25,000 fraudulent accounts in a “distillation” campaign. Alibaba did not immediately respond to Reuters’ request for comment.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/world/china/anthropic-says-alibaba-illicitly-extracted-claude-ai-model-capabilities-2026-06-24/)

3. EU joins U.S.-led Pax Silica initiative. The European Commission joined the U.S.-led “Pax Silica” initiative to secure supply chains needed for artificial intelligence, including energy, critical minerals, high-end manufacturing, and AI models.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/technology/eu-joins-us-led-pax-silica-securing-ai-chip-supply-chains-2026-06-25/)

4. Cloud regulation becomes an AI issue in Europe. EU regulators said cloud infrastructure has become critical to AI and proposed bringing AWS and Microsoft Azure under stricter Digital Markets Act obligations.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/retail-consumer/amazon-microsoft-cloud-computing-services-should-fall-under-eu-tech-rules-eu-2026-06-25/)

5. IBM’s chip announcement focuses on AI computing needs. IBM framed its sub-1-nanometer chip technology as part of the race to build semiconductors capable of handling heavier AI workloads with better power efficiency.

Source: [Reuters](https://www.reuters.com/business/ibm-unveils-tech-chip-smaller-than-1-nanometer-ai-computing-push-2026-06-25/)
