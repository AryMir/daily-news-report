Executive Summary

  • U.S. hiring slowed sharply in June, with employers adding 57,000 jobs, well below expectations, while unemployment fell to 4.2% because many people left the labor force. (Reuters)
  • Wall Street traded mixed after the jobs report, with the Dow higher but technology and semiconductor weakness weighing on the Nasdaq. (Reuters)
  • Oil fell to a four-month low after U.S.-Iran talks in Doha reduced immediate concern about shipping through the Strait of Hormuz. (Reuters)
  • Russia launched one of the war’s largest attacks on Kyiv, killing at least 21 people and damaging roughly 130 buildings. (Reuters)
  • Henderson, Nevada is sunny and very hot today, with a current temperature near 92°F and a forecast high near 103°F. (Weather forecast)

U.S. National News

  1. U.S. job growth slowed sharply in June. The Labor Department reported 57,000 new nonfarm payroll jobs, compared with economists’ expectations for 110,000, while April and May were revised down by a combined 74,000 jobs. (Reuters)
  2. The unemployment rate fell for a weak reason. Unemployment dropped to 4.2%, but Reuters reported that the decline was driven by about 720,000 people leaving the labor force, pushing participation to its lowest level since March 2021. (Reuters)
  3. Supreme Court upheld birthright citizenship earlier this week. The Court rejected President Trump’s executive order that sought to limit citizenship for children born in the United States to parents who are in the country illegally or temporarily. (AP)
  4. A federal judge blocked proposed USPS mail-in voting restrictions. Judge Emmet Sullivan ruled that the Postal Service’s proposed changes violated a 2021 settlement requiring expedited handling of mail ballots through 2028. (Reuters)
  5. The U.S. declined to extend the USMCA trade agreement in its current form. The decision starts a 10-year countdown toward expiration while the administration seeks changes to the North American trade pact. (Reuters)

International News

  1. Russia bombarded Kyiv in one of the war’s largest strikes. Ukrainian officials said Russia launched 74 missiles and 496 drones, killing at least 21 people, wounding more than 90, and damaging around 130 buildings. (Reuters)
  2. U.S.-Iran talks in Doha concluded without a lasting peace breakthrough. The indirect talks focused on maritime traffic in the Strait of Hormuz and Iranian funds, with Qatar describing “positive progress” but no broader settlement. (Reuters)
  3. Iran is preparing funeral events for Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. Reuters reported that state funeral events are planned to begin in Tehran and continue with ceremonies in Qom, Mashhad, and Iraq. (Reuters)
  4. Northern Nigeria faces its worst hunger levels in nearly a decade. The World Food Programme said more than 17 million people across nine conflict-affected states face acute food insecurity. (Reuters)
  5. European officials raised concern over China’s ethnic unity law. The European Union warned that the law’s overseas reach could restrict cultural, religious, and linguistic rights for groups including Tibetans and Uyghurs. (Reuters)

Technology Industry News

  1. Google lost its final appeal against a major EU Android fine. Europe’s top court dismissed Google’s challenge to a €4.1 billion antitrust fine tied to Android agreements that favored Google services. (Reuters)
  2. Meta is reportedly building a cloud business around excess AI capacity. Reuters, citing Bloomberg News, reported that Meta may sell access to AI models or raw computing power as it looks for returns on expensive AI infrastructure. (Reuters)
  3. Microsoft is reportedly preparing another round of layoffs. Reuters, citing Business Insider, reported that Microsoft plans to cut less than 2.5% of its workforce as technology firms continue to manage costs while investing heavily in AI. (Reuters)
  4. Europe’s chip sector faces strategic pressure. A Reuters report said European semiconductor companies face risks from both China and the United States unless the EU strengthens competitiveness and reduces dependency. (Reuters)

Artificial Intelligence News

  1. Microsoft launched Microsoft Frontier Company with $2.5 billion in backing. The new entity is designed to help large customers choose, customize, and deploy AI tools for their own business workflows. (Reuters)
  2. The U.S. government is discussing voluntary AI model standards. Reuters, citing the Financial Times, reported that officials are in advanced talks with AI companies about standards for releasing new models. (Reuters)
  3. China’s Z.ai is gaining attention with a lower-cost AI model. Reuters reported that GLM-5.2, developed by Beijing-based Z.ai, is drawing users because of its coding and agentic performance at lower cost than many U.S. models. (Reuters)
  4. The UN warned about risks from unchecked AI development. A United Nations independent panel warned that advanced AI systems may create serious risks if oversight and evidence-gathering do not keep pace. (Reuters)
