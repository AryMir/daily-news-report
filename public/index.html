Executive Summary

Global oil prices saw mixed movements today, largely influenced by optimism surrounding a potential U.S.-Iran peace deal and the expected reopening of the Strait of Hormuz.

A new AP-NORC poll reveals that most Americans perceive civil liberties, such as the right to vote and freedom of speech, as being under threat.

Retailers nationwide reported faster-than-expected revenue growth in May, signaling a potentially solid consumer spending environment for the economy.

U.S. Senators Martin Heinrich and Andy Kim hosted a roundtable to address how the U.S.-Iran conflict has contributed to rising gas prices and impacted working families.

President Trump is at the G7 summit, touting a tentative agreement with Iran to end the conflict, with plans for the Strait of Hormuz to be fully reopened by Friday.

International News

U.S. President Donald Trump announced that the Strait of Hormuz is expected to be fully reopened by Friday and remain open permanently.

Leaders from Egypt, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates joined G7 discussions focused on stability in the Middle East and comprehensive regional settlements.

Brazil's Supreme Court sentenced former lawmaker Eduardo Bolsonaro to four years and two months in prison for coercion and illegally seeking U.S. government intervention.

G7 leaders, with partner countries Brazil and the Republic of Korea, issued a declaration committing to intensify the global fight against drug trafficking.

June 17, 2026

U.S. stock markets closed mixed on Tuesday as investors reacted to falling oil prices and awaited the Federal Reserve's policy decision. The Dow Jones saw gains, hitting a new record, while technology stocks weighed on the S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite. Oil prices continued to be volatile, primarily driven by expectations of an interim U.S.-Iran deal which could lead to increased global supply.

Technology Industry News

Applied Materials unveiled SENZ™, a fully integrated visual system designed to accelerate the development of next-generation AI-powered smart glasses.

Accenture announced its agreement to acquire Industries eXcellence Group, aiming to enhance its capabilities in modernizing manufacturing operations through software, data, and AI-enabled technologies.

SpaceX shares continued their rally following the company's announcement of acquiring AI coding assistant Cursor in a $60 billion deal.

Jabil reported stronger-than-expected quarterly results, attributing its performance to "extremely strong" demand for AI infrastructure.

Intel released its 2025–2026 Corporate Responsibility Report, highlighting advancements in responsible business practices and AI-driven growth.

Artificial Intelligence News

Amazon founder Jeff Bezos predicted that artificial intelligence will create labor shortages rather than replace human workers, speaking optimistically at the VivaTech conference.

Deloitte, in collaboration with Google Cloud, launched a new AI Studio in London to help UK businesses deploy autonomous, action-oriented "Agentic AI" solutions at scale.

Sabre Corporation announced a significant deployment of its agentic AI technology with Ultra Group (now Linex Travel), aimed at transforming global enterprise loyalty and travel servicing.

The AIFinTech100 list recognized leading firms redefining AI innovation in financial services, noting a major shift towards "agentic AI" in the sector.

Jet.AI is pivoting entirely to artificial intelligence, focusing on developing AI infrastructure and data centers, including a proposed data center project in Moapa, Nevada.
