Executive Summary

  • In technology, Apple raised Mac and iPad prices, Meta was sued by a former executive over efforts to suppress her memoir, and Qualcomm laid out a broader AI infrastructure push.

U.S. National News

  • Federal judge blocks Trump's federal voter-list order. AP reported Friday that U.S. District Judge Indira Talwani halted the administration's executive order seeking a national voter list and tighter mail-ballot limits, ruling that election-rule power belongs to states and Congress rather than the president.
  • Supreme Court lets the administration end TPS protections for Haitians and Syrians. AP reported the 6-3 ruling allows the administration to terminate Temporary Protected Status for about 356,000 people from Haiti and Syria, with broader implications for other TPS-designated groups.
  • Supreme Court strikes down Hawaii's gun-permission law for private businesses. In a 6-3 decision, the justices said Hawaii cannot require gun owners to get permission before carrying firearms into places such as stores and hotels; property owners can still ban guns themselves.
  • Supreme Court sides with Bayer in the Roundup labeling fight. AP reported the 7-2 ruling blocks thousands of state-law suits claiming Roundup should have carried a cancer warning because federal regulators did not require one on the label.
  • The 988 LGBTQ+ youth line is set to relaunch, but The Trevor Project may be excluded. AP reported Friday that the administration plans to restart the specialized 988 option later this year after Congress provided $33 million, but current participation rules could bar the nonprofit that helped build the service.

International News

  • Venezuela's earthquake toll climbed again as rescue work continued. AP reported Friday that the back-to-back 7.2 and 7.5 quakes killed at least 589 people and injured about 2,980, with a state of emergency still in effect.
  • Ukraine launched one of its heaviest drone bombardments of Russia. AP reported Russia said it intercepted 660 Ukrainian drones overnight across 12 regions and occupied Crimea, while both sides also exchanged 160 prisoners of war.
  • South Korea's former first lady was sentenced to seven years in prison. AP reported the Seoul Central District Court convicted Kim Keon Hee over luxury gifts tied to political and business favors, adding to earlier criminal trouble surrounding the former presidential couple.
  • A U.N. maritime agency paused ship evacuations through the Strait of Hormuz. AP reported the move followed an attack on a vessel off Oman, underscoring that shipping risks remain elevated even as traders watch for more stable oil flows.
  • Japan reported a strong offshore earthquake with no tsunami warning. AP reported a 7.2-magnitude quake struck off northern Japan, with authorities saying there was no tsunami danger and no immediate report of serious injuries.

Technology Industry News

  • Apple raised Mac and iPad prices because of memory-chip costs. AP reported Friday that Apple blamed a global memory shortage tied to AI data-center demand, and said some Mac prices rose by $100 to $300 while some iPad prices rose by about $150 to $200.
  • A former Meta executive sued the company over her memoir. AP reported Friday that Sarah Wynn-Williams says Meta is unlawfully trying to silence her book Careless People through arbitration and severance restrictions, while Meta says she is spreading falsehoods.
  • Xbox is raising console prices worldwide on August 1. Xbox Wire said Thursday that 512 GB consoles will rise by $100, 1 TB models by $150, and the 2 TB model will be discontinued.
  • Qualcomm said it plans to acquire Modular. Qualcomm said June 24 that the deal is meant to strengthen its developer-first AI software stack and help scale generative and agentic AI from edge devices to data centers.

Artificial Intelligence News

  • A new U.S. workforce group launched with more than $500 million to address AI job disruption. AP reported Thursday that the bipartisan nonprofit RAISE US will begin pilot programs with states and employers including Amazon, Microsoft, Bank of America, and IBM.
  • Qualcomm expanded its AI infrastructure push with new partners and a broader roadmap. Qualcomm said June 24 that it has a multi-generation data-center CPU agreement with Meta and an expanded relationship with Hugging Face aimed at open, developer-driven AI from device to cloud.
  • Federal regulators ordered grid operators to speed power access for AI data centers. AP reported last week that the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission told major grid operators to move faster on connecting large, power-hungry projects needed for AI expansion.
  • U.N. Secretary-General Antonio Guterres called for more AI environmental disclosure. AP reported Tuesday that he wants AI companies to publish their environmental impact and commit to renewable electricity for their facilities by 2030.
